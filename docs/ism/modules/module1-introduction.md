# Module 1: Introduction to Information Systems

## Overview

Information Systems (IS) are combinations of hardware, software, data, people, and procedures that work together to process data and provide information to support organizational decision-making.

## 1. What is an Information System?

### Definition

An **Information System** is an organized combination of:
- **Hardware:** Physical components (computers, servers, networks)
- **Software:** Programs and applications
- **Data:** Raw facts and figures
- **People:** Users and IT professionals
- **Procedures:** Rules and guidelines for operation

## 2. Types of Information Systems

### Transaction Processing Systems (TPS)

**Purpose:** Process day-to-day transactions

**Characteristics:**
- High volume of transactions
- Real-time processing
- Data accuracy critical
- Examples: Point-of-sale systems, payroll systems

### Management Information Systems (MIS)

**Purpose:** Provide reports for management decision-making

**Characteristics:**
- Summarized data from TPS
- Periodic reports (daily, weekly, monthly)
- Structured decision support
- Examples: Sales reports, inventory reports

### Decision Support Systems (DSS)

**Purpose:** Support semi-structured and unstructured decisions

**Characteristics:**
- Interactive systems
- "What-if" analysis
- Model-based
- Examples: Financial planning systems, forecasting tools

### Executive Information Systems (EIS)

**Purpose:** Provide strategic information to top management

**Characteristics:**
- High-level summaries
- Graphical displays
- Drill-down capability
- Examples: Executive dashboards

### Expert Systems (ES)

**Purpose:** Mimic human expertise in specific domains

**Characteristics:**
- Rule-based reasoning
- Knowledge base
- Inference engine
- Examples: Medical diagnosis systems, troubleshooting guides

## 3. System Components

### Input

- Data entry devices
- Sensors
- Scanners
- Keyboards

### Processing

- CPU operations
- Data transformation
- Calculations
- Decision logic

### Output

- Reports
- Displays
- Alerts
- Documents

### Storage

- Primary storage (RAM)
- Secondary storage (Hard drives, SSDs)
- Database systems

### Feedback

- System monitoring
- Error messages
- Performance metrics
- User feedback

---

## 4. Business Information Systems

### Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)

**Purpose:** Integrate all business processes

**Modules:**
- Finance
- Human Resources
- Supply Chain
- Manufacturing
- Sales & Marketing

### Customer Relationship Management (CRM)

**Purpose:** Manage customer interactions

**Functions:**
- Contact management
- Sales tracking
- Customer service
- Marketing automation

### Supply Chain Management (SCM)

**Purpose:** Manage flow of goods and services

**Components:**
- Procurement
- Inventory management
- Logistics
- Distribution
